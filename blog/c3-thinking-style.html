Learning Styles

Maximizing strengths, realizing weaknesses, and planning for growth

December 5, 2014

Looking at Anthony Gregorc’s Mind Styles Model, my personal thinking style is that of “abstract random”. This means that I learn best when I’m given broad or general guidelines and am able to participate in group activities, and I struggle with concentrating on one thing at a time, competition, giving exact details, and accepting even positive criticism. I know I’ll have to work through that last difficulty, as giving and receiving feedback is a vital part of DBC. I’ve noticed in the past that when I’ve attempted to critique someone, it often blows up into something more, which leads me to believe I’m not very good at providing criticism, either. Hopefully practice will help me improve how I phrase my critiques.

In addition to providing feedback to one another, we are encouraged to work with others, which is an immense help to me. I’ve been struggling to keep up so far, and have been hesitant to work with others, as I don’t want to waste their time. Whenever I’ve paired with someone else in DBC, however, I’ve gotten so much more out of the material. It helps me to talk concepts through with someone else to really solidify them in my mind. When working by myself, I can often get stuck on a question that will lead me to spend way too much time researching the answer, which I could often reach sooner by just asking a member of my cohort. I’m looking forward to reaching out more and hopefully meeting up with members of my cohort in person for study sessions.
